Project Description

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that primarily affects memory, thinking skills, and behavior. It is the most common cause of dementia, a syndrome characterized by a decline in cognitive function severe enough to interfere with daily life.

Non-Demented: Individuals in this stage exhibit normal

cognitive function, with no signs of memory loss

impairment in thinking skills.are able to perform daily

tasks independently and engage in social activities

without difficulty.

Very Mild Demented (Stage 1): Individuals in

stage exhibit normal cognitive function, with no signs of

memory loss impairment in thinking skills.are able to

daily tasks independently and engage in social activities

without difficulty.

Mild Demented (Stage 2): Individuals in this stage may

start to exhibit mild cognitive decline. While they can

still perform daily tasks independently, they may begin

to experience occasional memory lapses and subtle

changes in thinking skills.

Moderate Demented (Stage 3 ): At this stage, cognitive

decline becomes more noticeable and pronounced.

Individuals may experience moderate memory loss,

including forgetting recent events or important personal

information.
